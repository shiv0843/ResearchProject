Our Research & Insights

Our work is founded on a commitment to understanding the evolving landscape of digital privacy through ongoing research and data analysis.

Excessive Permissions

Our studies show that over 80% of free apps request permissions beyond what is functionally necessary. This often includes access to the microphone and camera for apps that have no apparent need for them, raising significant security concerns.

Hidden Trackers

We've discovered that many popular apps, particularly those in the social media and gaming categories, contain hidden third-party trackers. These trackers silently collect user data, behavior, and demographics for targeted advertising and data brokers.

Background Data Collection

A surprising number of applications continue to access and transmit data in the background even when not in active use. This can include location data, device identifiers, and contact lists, which can lead to privacy leaks if not properly monitored.

App-to-App Communication

Our research into inter-app communication shows that many apps share data with each other without explicit user consent. A simple utility app, for instance, might be able to read data from a messaging app, creating a potential backdoor for data breaches.
